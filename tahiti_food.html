We are travelling around the islands of Polynesia.

Sunday brunch, Tahitian style

Every Sunday, streets line up with people grilling meat and selling the local firi firi donuts. In the photo, we added some island-grown papaya, and mango juice produced in Moorea, an island we see from our window.

Also, it's 2020 - when you edit out stains on the table from the photo quicker than you get round to actually cleaning them. ;D

Poisson Cru, the royal gem of Polynesian cuisine

Red tuna steak, coconut oil, lime, onion, tomato. (I skip the cucumber.) That's pretty much the making for Poisson Cru - a raw, refreshing dish that's so filling and full of flavor it may as well be crack.

Notice Hinano beer- produced on Tahiti, and delicious. Hinano is hands down the most important Polynesian brand.

Grilled fish with vanilla sauce

Another one that will make you close your eyes in delight. The islands have plenty of vanilla farms. And they put it to incredible use. This one dish is un-real.
